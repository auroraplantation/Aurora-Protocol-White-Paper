# Why Aurora

> **Aurora Protocol is purpose-built for agricultural RWA financing — combining DeFi infrastructure with real-world verification to deliver what generic lending protocols cannot.**

---

## Core Differentiators

| Differentiator | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Agriculture-First Design** | Every protocol component — from batch structure to milestone gating — is designed around agricultural production cycles, not generic loan terms |
| **5-Layer Verification** | Multi-layered due diligence combining identity, agricultural data, financials, physical inspection, and ongoing monitoring (see [Verification](../Protocol/Verification.md)) |
| **Milestone-Gated Disbursement** | Funds are released incrementally through `EscrowVault`'s 7-state machine — not in a single lump sum |
| **Burn-to-Claim Settlement** | Clean, one-time redemption mechanism that eliminates double-claim risk and ensures verifiable settlement |
| **Southeast Asia Focus** | Deep regional knowledge, local inspection partners, and on-the-ground operational presence |
| **Transparent Fee Model** | Single platform fee (≤ 3%) — no hidden fees, no performance charges, no management fees for investors |

## Why Now

Several converging trends make this the right moment for Aurora Protocol:

| Trend | Implication for Aurora |
|-------|------------------------|
| **RWA narrative maturity** | Institutional and retail attention is shifting from speculative DeFi to real-yield, asset-backed protocols |
| **Regulatory clarity** | Hong Kong and other jurisdictions are establishing clearer frameworks for tokenized securities and digital assets |
| **USDC adoption** | Stablecoin infrastructure is mature enough for reliable cross-border settlement |
| **Ethereum scalability** | Layer 2 solutions and lower gas costs make per-batch deployment economically viable |
| **Post-pandemic food security focus** | Governments and multilaterals are prioritizing agricultural supply chain resilience |
